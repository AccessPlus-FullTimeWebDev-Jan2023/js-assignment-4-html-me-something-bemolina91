A Trip Through Patagonia

El Calafate

Day one I arrived to El Calafate, Argentina, which just so happened to also be the day of the final of the World Cup in which Argentina was playing and won! Most of the day was spent celebrating with the entire town, and then I wound down with a beautiful, evening stroll along Lago Argentino.

Glaciar Perito Moreno

Day two was spent visiting the famous Perito Moreno Glaciar, which was one of the coolest sights I've ever seen. I took a boat tour out to the base of the glaciar, blazed a trail around the lake, and took a frigid polar plunge next to a small iceberg.

Parque Nacional Torres del Paine

Day three started with a road trip to the Chilean side of Patagonia to visit Torres del Paine National Park. The mountains and the interesting structure of the towers (torres) were unlike any I had ever seen before. One day was not enough time to explore this amazing park, and I cannot wait to go back.

El Chalten

El Chalten was easily my favorite part of all of Patagonia. The views every step of the way were beyond spectacular. In two days I hiked 33 miles, climbed over 9,000 feet, and earned sixteen blisters for my efforts, but each excruciatingly painful step I took was 100% worth it.

Back to top

Ushuaia

My last days in Patagonia were spent in Ushuaia, Argentina, which is the southernmost city in the world. There I explored the beautiful, seaside town, took a boat tour to Isla Martillo and spent Christmas day with the penguins, and hiked a few beautiful trails in Tierra del Fuego National Park. I couldn't imagine a better ending to my trip.
